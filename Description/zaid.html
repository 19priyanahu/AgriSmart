Zaid Crops

Rabi crops, also known as Rabi fasal, are cultivated in India during the winter season and harvested in the spring. The word “Rabi” is derived from the Arabic word for “spring,” and in the Indian subcontinent it refers to spring or winter crops. These crops grow well in cooler climates and require less water compared to Kharif crops. Major Rabi crops in India include cereals such as wheat, barley, and oats; pulses like gram (chana); oilseeds such as mustard and linseed; and spices and other crops including garlic, cumin, and coriander.


In India, major Rabi crops such as wheat, mustard, and peas are harvested early, while the overall Rabi season extends into spring. These crops, including wheat, barley, and oats (cereals), gram or chana (pulses), linseed, and mustard, are typically sown in winter and harvested in spring. Rabi crops can also be classified into different categories based on their usage, such as food crops, fodder crops, fiber crops, oil crops, ornamental crops, and industrial crops.
