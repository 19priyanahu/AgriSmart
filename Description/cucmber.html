  • Cucumber is a popular Zaid crop cultivated mainly during the hot summer months. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is known for its refreshing fruits consumed raw in salads, pickles, and beverages. Its fast growth cycle and short duration (60–75 days) make it ideal for summer cultivation. India is one of the leading producers, and demand rises significantly during the hot months due to its cooling effect on the body.

  • Cucumber grows best in warm climates with an ideal temperature range of 20°C–30°C. It requires long days and plenty of sunshine for proper flowering and fruit development. It is highly sensitive to frost and does not perform well if temperatures drop below 15°C. On the other hand, extremely high temperatures above 35°C can reduce flower formation and fruit quality. Therefore, the summer months (March–June) are most suitable in India.

  • Cucumber requires moderate irrigation, but soil moisture must be maintained evenly, especially during flowering and fruit development. Water stress at this stage leads to bitter fruits and reduced yield. On average, cucumbers need 350–500 mm of water in their growing cycle. Irrigation should be done every 6–8 days in summer. Modern farmers often use drip irrigation with mulching to save water and keep fruits clean.

  • Cucumber is primarily a Zaid crop, sown in March–April after the Rabi harvest, and harvested by May–June. In irrigated areas, cucumbers can also be cultivated in other seasons, but Zaid is the peak period because of high market demand. In hilly areas, it is also grown during the summer months when temperatures are favorable.

  • Cucumber is 95% water, making it hydrating and low in calories. It contains Vitamin K, Vitamin C, potassium, and antioxidants. Regular consumption helps digestion, controls hydration, and supports skin health. Economically, cucumber fetches premium prices in summer markets due to its refreshing quality. It is also an important ingredient in the pickle industry, increasing its value beyond fresh consumption.
